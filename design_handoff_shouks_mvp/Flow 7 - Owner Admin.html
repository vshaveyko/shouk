Rules & policies

The scaffolding that keeps Air-Cooled Ferraris curated. Members see these on the application form and on listing creation — changes apply to new submissions only.

What can be listed

Only air-cooled Ferraris (1948–1998)
Any F-badged vehicle with a water-cooled V8/V12 will be auto-flagged for review. Parts & memorabilia allowed under separate categories.
Titles must be clear or disclosed as salvage
Members cannot list a car with a hidden salvage history. Failure results in immediate removal.
Seller must own the car listed
No "broker" listings on behalf of anonymous owners — the member on the listing must be the titled owner.
No cross-listings on other auction platforms
Currently disabled — hard to enforce and some members push back.

Marketplace settings

The rarely-touched bits. URL, visibility, and how the marketplace winds down.

General

Marketplace URL

Your public address on Shouks. Changing it breaks existing links.
shouks.com /

Contact email

Where application inquiries and Shouks notices go.

Timezone

Used for auction close times, digest emails.

Visibility

Who can discover and browse your marketplace.

Changes take effect immediately for new visitors. Existing members keep their access either way.

Switching from Public → Closed or Private will immediately hide listings from non-members and remove your marketplace from Explore. Indexed URLs may still redirect visitors to your landing page for up to 24 hours.

Danger zone

Transfer ownership

Make another admin the Creator. You become an admin; the new Creator can undo this.

Archive marketplace

Hide from public view, pause applications. Members keep read-only access to their own history. Reversible.

Delete marketplace

All listings, members, and messages are permanently deleted after a 30-day grace period. This cannot be undone.
NotesSettings is the "rare edit" surface, so it's quiet and form-heavy. Visibility is promoted to its own panel — it's the highest-stakes lever on this page and mirrors the Public / Closed / Private choice creators saw during marketplace creation. Danger zone is visually distinct (red header, red destructive button) but not alarming — archive and transfer are both recoverable.
